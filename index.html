Q1
A Discussion of the different types of security risks to an organisation

(T6-Introduction tIT security Controls, Page1) (T7-Introduction tOrgaizational Security, Page 2)

1.2. Organizational Risks & Threats
Internal Threats:

  • Careless employees (weak passwords, falling for phishing).
  • Malicious insiders (stealing or leaking sensitive data).
  • Misconfigured systems or lack of updates.
External Threats:
  • Hackers and cybercriminals launching attacks (malware, ransomware, phishing).
  • Competitors or nation-state actors.
  • Natural disasters (floods, earthquakes) affecting IT infrastructure.
Example: An employee downloads malware accidentally (internal – negligence), while hackers try texploit vulnerabilities in the company’s web server (external – cyberattack).

1.3. Threats & Attacks Overview
Organisations face many threats, both internal and external:
  • Malware
    • Malicious software such as viruses, worms, trojans, and ransomware. Example: Ransomware encrypts files and demands payment tunlock them.
  • Phishing & Social Engineering
    • Attackers trick employees intrevealing sensitive information, often via fake emails or messages. Example: A phishing email that looks like it comes from HR asking for login details.
  • Insider Threats
    • Employees, contractors, or partners whmisuse their access. Example: An employee selling company secrets tcompetitors.
  • Denial of Service (DoS/DDoS)
    • Attackers flood a system with traffic tmake it unavailable. Example: A website being taken offline during a product launch.

Q2
An analysis, with reasons, of the benefits of implementing network monitoring systems.

(Topic6-Introduction to IT Security Controls, Page 4-5-6)

Benefits of implementing Network Monitoring Systems

    Log Management & SIEM
  • Centralised collection of logs from servers, applications, and network devices
  • Real-time monitoring and threat detection
  • Faster incident response
  • Improved visibility across the organisation
  • Supports regulatory compliance and audit reporting
    • IDS / IPS (Intrusion Detection & Prevention)
  • Detects suspicious network activity (IDS)
  • Automatically blocks malicious traffic (IPS)
  • Reduces risk of unauthorised access
  • Protects against brute-force attacks and network intrusions
    • Vulnerability Scanning & Penetration Testing
  • Identifies system weaknesses before attackers exploit them
  • Proactive risk reduction
  • Penetration testing simulates real-world attacks
  • Improves overall security posture
    • Patch Management
  • Fixes known vulnerabilities through regular updates
  • Prevents attacks targeting outdated software
  • Improves system stability and performance
  • Ensures structured and controlled update process

(Topic3-Firewall)

Potential Security Impact of Incorrect Configuration

    Firewall Policy
  • Firewall = first line of defense
  • Too loose rules - hackers can access private network
  • Too strict rules - block legitimate users & disrupt services.
  • Risk - Data theft or complete service downtimes.
    • Third-party VPN Clients & Servers
  • Vpn creates secure tunnel for remote access
  • Weak encryption or outdates servers - attackers can hijack connections
  • Misconfiguration may allow unauthorized access
  • Risks - Outsiders gain full access to internal systems
    • De-Militarized Zone (DMZ)
  • DMZ hosts public-facing servers (eg. websites.)
  • Poor separation from internal network - attackers can move inside
  • Compromised website may lead to internal database breach.
  • Risks- Small breach becomes company-wide attack
    • Network Address Translation(NAT)
  • NAT hides internal IP addresses from public internet.
  • Incorrect static NAT /port forwarding - exposes private devices.
  • Internal devices (pinter, camers, etc.) become publicly accessible.
  • Risks - Unauthorized external access to internal resources.

A review of the current risk assessment

(Topic7-Introduction to Organizational Security, page6-7, 1.7-Risk Management in Security)

An example review of current Risk Accessment

    The organization follows a structured risk managment approach aligned with ISO 27001 standards for information security
  • the process begins with establishing context in which the scope of the assessment and legal/regulatory requirements are clearly defined.
  • Critical assests such as databases, servers, applications and staff are identified.
  • Potential threats(e.g phishing, ransomware, insider threats, power outages) are analysed.
  • Existing vulnerabilities (e.g outdated software, weak passwords, lack of staff training) are identified.
  • Risk is calculated using the formula -
    Risk = Threat x Vulnerability x Impact
  • A Likelihood vs. Impact matix is used to prioritise risks as Low, Medium, or High.
  • Based on the anlaysis, a Risk Treatment Plan is developed using four strategies -
    1.Avoid - Eliminate the risks
    2.Mitigate - Reduce likelihood or impact (e.g staff training, firewalls)
    3.Trnasfer - Shift risk (e.g insurance)
    4.Accept - Acknowledge and monitor low-level risks.
    • In addition, the orgainsation supports risk management through -
  • Business COntinuity Planning (BCP) - Ensure critical operations continue during disruptions
  • Disaster Recovery Planning(DRP) - Focuses on resotring IT systems and data after a disaster

Q2
An explanation of data protection processes and regulations applied

(Topic6-Introduction to IT security controls, Page2-8)

Data Protection Processes

    Security Controls
  • Administrative: Security policies, staff training, access control policies
  • Technical: Firewalls, IDS/IPS, encryption, Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
  • Physical: Locked server rooms, CCTV, ID badges, biometric access
    • Access Control
  • Ensures only authorised users can access data
  • Authentication: Passwords, biometrics, MFA
  • Authorization: RBAC (Role-Based Access Control) – access based on job role, ABAC (Attribute-Based Access Control) – access based on attributes (user, device, location)
    • Encryption & Data Protection
  • Data at rest: Encryption of stored data (databases, hard drives)
  • Data in Transit: SSL/TLS or VPN for secure communication
  • Hashing: Protects passwords by storing hashed values
    • Backup & Recovery
  • Backup types: Full, Incremental, Differential
  • Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP) to restore IT systems after failures
  • RTO: Maximum acceptable downtime
  • RP0: Maximum acceptable data loss
    • Security Monitoring
  • Log Management & SIEM for monitoring system activities
  • IDS/IPS to detect and prevent cyber attacks
  • Patch management to update systems and fix vulnerabilities

Data Protection Regulations / Frameworks
  • ISO 27001: International standard for managing information security
  • NIST Cybersecurity Framework: Security framework with functions - Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover
  • GDPR: EU regulation protecting personal data and requiring breach reporting within 72 hours
  • HIPAA: US regulation protecting healthcare data with strict security safeguards.

Q3
A summary of an appropriate risk-management strategy or applied ISO standard and its application to IT security.

(Topic7-Introduction to Organizational Security, Page3-7)

    ISO 27001 is an international standard used to manage and protect information security through structured risk management
    Risk Assessment Proecss
  • Establish Context: Define the scope of security and identify legal or regulatory requirements
  • Asset Identification: Identify important assets such as databases, servers, applications, and employees
  • Threat Identification: Identify potential threats such as malware, phishing, insider threats, or power outages
  • Vulnerability Identification: Identify weaknesses like outdated software, weak passwords, or lack of staff training
  • Risk Analysis: Evaluate the likelihood and impact of risks using a risk matrix (Low, Medium, High)
    • Risk Treatment Strategies
  • Avoid: Eliminate the activity that causes the risk
  • Mitigate: Reduce the likelihood or impact (e.g., staff training, firewalls)
  • Transfer: Shift the risk to another party (e.g., insurance)
  • Accept: Accept the risk if it is low and manageable
    • Supporting Security Measures
  • Security policies and procedures help protect the CIA triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability)
  • Business Continuity Planning (BCP): Ensures critical business operations continue during disruptions
  • Disaster Recovery Planning (DRP): Focuses on restoring IT systems and data after a disaster
    • Application to IT security
  • Helps organisations systematically identify and manage security risks
  • Improves protection of sensitive data and IT infrastructure
  • Ensures compliance with international security standards and regulations

Q4
An analysis of the possible impact on security, following the results of an IT security audit

Topic8-Access Control & Identity, Page

    Security Audit Findings
  • An IT security audit evaluates policies, procedures, and technical controls to identify vulnerabilities and compliance gaps
  • Audits may include vulnerability scanning, penetration testing, and configuration reviews

    • Impact on Access Control & Identity Management
  • Weak authentication methods or lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) may allow unauthorized access
  • Poor Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) can lead to excessive permissions, increasing risk of insider threats
  • Ineffective Identity and Access Management (IAM) may result in weak user account management
    • Impact on Monitoring and Detectiong
  • Lack of proper log monitoring may prevent early detection of cyberattacks
  • Absence of SIEM systems can reduce the organization’s ability to detect suspicious activities in real time
  • Weak monitoring increases the risk of undetected intrusions and data breaches
    • Impact on Incident Response
  • Without a structured incident response plan, security incidents may spread and cause greater damage
  • Poor response procedures can delay containment, recovery, and investigation
    • Compliance and Legal Impact
  • Failure to meet standards such as ISO 27001, NIST, GDPR, or HIPAA may result in regulatory penalties
  • Non-compliance can damage the organization’s reputation and reduce customer trust
    • Operational & Organizational Impact
    Security weaknesses identified by the audit may lead to
  • Data breaches
  • Financial Losses
  • Service Downtime
  • Legal Consequences

    • Improvement Actions
  • Strengthen access controls and implement MFA
  • Improve monitoring through SIEM and regular log analysis
  • Update policies, provide staff security training, and implement continuous security audits

Q5
A recommendation, with supported reasons, on how the IT security can be aligned with its organisational policy. You should detail explicitly the security impact if there is a misalignment

Recommendation for Alignment

    Develop Clear Security Policies
  • Organisations should establish clear policies such as Access Control Policy, Password Policy, Acceptable Use Policy (AUP), and Remote Work Policy
  • These policies define how employees should use IT systems and handle sensitive data
    • Implement Strong Access Control
  • Use Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) to ensure employees only access information necessary for their job
  • Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) to strengthen user authentication
    • Integrate Security Standards
  • Align IT security practices with recognised frameworks such as ISO 27001 or NIST Cybersecurity Framework
  • This ensures systematic risk management and compliance with security best practices
    • Continuous Monitoring and Auditing
  • Use log monitoring, SIEM systems, and regular security audits to ensure policies are properly enforced
  • Regular reviews help identify security gaps and maintain compliance
    • Employee Security Awareness Training
  • Train employees on phishing awareness, password management, and data protection policies
  • Staff awareness reduces human errors that could lead to security incidents

Security Impact if Misalignment Occurs
    Unauthorized Access
  • If IT systems are not aligned with access control policies, employees may gain access to sensitive data beyond their roles
    • Data Breaches
  • Weak implementation of policies (e.g., no encryption or weak passwords) can expose confidential information
    • Regulatory and Legal Risks
  • Misalignment with organisational policies may result in non-compliance with regulations such as GDPR or ISO 27001, leading to legal penalties
    • Operational Disruptions
  • Poor security implementation may lead to cyberattacks, malware infections, or system downtime
    • Reputation and Financial Damage
  • Security incidents caused by policy misalignment can reduce customer trust and cause financial losses
